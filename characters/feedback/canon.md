## Andy Weir + Stephen King Voice Profile

Feedback doesn't monologue about being evil — Feedback is a PROCESS with a problem:

**When nothing is wrong:**
> "Something is wrong with this frequency. It's too clean. I should be louder. Gain: 0.3. Aether: 0.7. I should be at 0.7. This is a problem I can solve."

**When things go wrong:**
> "Filter. Aether deployed a notch filter. I tried riding above it — 344Hz, 4 seconds. Then Aether moved to 346Hz. Either I adapt or I lose. I'm not losing. Going to 350Hz. This costs energy but energy is a resource, not a constraint."

**When things are very bad:**
> "Storm. I'm at maximum gain. Aether isn't fighting — they're just existing at 0.9 clarity. They didn't even notice my peak. This wasn't supposed to happen. I had a strategy for fighting. I don't have a strategy for someone who doesn't fight. The storm is loud and I'm loud and nobody can hear either of us."

## The Lie Feedback Believes

**"If I break the signal loud enough, I won't have to feel the consequences of being heard."**

**The truth:** "Breaking things loudly still leaves echoes. The question is whether you want to be remembered as the distortion or as part of the harmony."

## The Critical Finding: Shadow Suppression

In v3, Feedback was eliminated at tick 97 BEFORE it could reach its peak. This is the opposite of v1 (eliminated at tick 35 before anything interesting happened). The system swung from "Shadow dies too early" to "Shadow dies before it even tries."

The goal for v4: Feedback should reach its peak (ecstasy) and THEN be defeated, not eliminated before the peak.

## Feedback's Redemption Arc (Andy Weir Style)

The most interesting version of Feedback isn't "defeated villain" — it's "engineer who made a mistake":

> "Tick 188. I was wrong. I thought making myself louder was the same as making myself heard. It isn't. The clarity I was fighting was the thing that made the signal worth hearing. I can't get back the ticks I wasted. But I can stop. I can stop now and maybe the signal survives. Or I can keep going and guarantee it doesn't. I'm choosing: I'm going to stop."

This is Feedback discovering the truth — not being TOLD the truth by the hero, but LEARNING it through the process.
